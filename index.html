GitHub crates.io Nymtech
--Nymnet--
Implements de-facto sender anonymity via SURBs.
--Nym Clients--
Connect Deonym nodes to the Nymnet.
--Deonym Clients--
Send their message to a Wingman
--Deonym Wingmen--
Buffer messages from clients
& answer delegation requests
--Deonym Services--
Asks Wingmen for delegations
Nym addresses
In this setup, only the Nym address of the Wingman needs to be public;
All services are senders covered by Nym's sender anonymity.
Deon addresses
Services are internally identified by their RSA public key.
This allows physical address uniqueness, zero-lookup encryption, and secure delegation.
Tracking
Not all services are present - "seed" - on all Wingmen.
Clients remember which Wingmen worked for which service,
can look up the network topology at central trackers,
and discover new nodes through various other decentralized means.
Stateless & Stateful
Always going all the way over the Wingman route not only doubles the amount of
Nym hops needed, but also requires asymmetric encryption of every single message.
However, it is entirely possible to only use the full, stateless, route, to
initiate a stateful connection that can directly communicate with the service.
SURB hoisting
Deonym needs SURBs where Nym doesn't support them, requiring SURBs to travel a protocol up where neccessary.
This requires nodes to generate SURBs by dynamically sending messages to themselves.
SURB hoisting is neccessary for all surbian PUT requests and stateful PUT responses.
However, sending arbitrarily many SURBs to an untrusted third party could deanonymize the recipient.
The safety of stateful connections is dependent on how Nym deals with this, and therefore they are disabled by default.
Up a protocol layer
Deonym standardizes communication with the Deon-protocol. It has two methods:
PUT-Frames for payloads and SURBs & POP-Frames for delegations.
Security
Deonym wants to achieve higher security by changing a service's role from receiver to sender on Nym layer.
While this should be inherently safer, (see Nymtech), I will not speak any guarantees.
